# Sales Engineer 30-60-90 Day Plan

> **Philosophy:** "I'm not hiring you to do work. I'm hiring you to solve problems. Here are your problems to solve in 30, 60, and 90 days." - Alex Hormozi

This plan outlines the **problems you need to solve** - not tasks to complete. Your job is to figure out HOW to solve them creatively and efficiently.

---

## Days 1-30: Foundation & Early Wins

### Core Problem to Solve

**Build trust with first customers and establish credible technical expertise.**

**Why this matters:**
- You need proof you can have intelligent conversations with engineers
- Early wins create momentum and confidence
- You're learning what works (and what doesn't)

### Success Metrics

**Activity Metrics:**
- [ ] 50+ leads contacted (personalized outreach)
- [ ] 10+ qualified conversations (15+ min discovery)
- [ ] 2+ meetings scheduled (technical deep-dive)
- [ ] 1+ proposal submitted (with engineering support)

**Quality Metrics:**
- Email response rate >= 15%
- Leads agree to second conversation >= 40%
- Can handle basic technical questions without escalation

### How You'll Spend Your Time

**Daily Activities (approximate):**
- **Research** (1-1.5 hours): Deep-dive 5 target companies
  - Website, products, recent news
  - Decision maker LinkedIn profiles
  - Competitive landscape
  
- **Outreach** (2 hours): Send 10 personalized emails
  - Use template library
  - Heavy customization required
  - Document what messages work
  
- **Follow-up** (30 min): 5 previous contacts
  - Track in CRM
  - Add value in every touchpoint
  
- **Learning** (1 hour): Product knowledge, competitors, industry trends

**Weekly:**
- Pipeline review with manager (30 min)
- Update CRM with all activities
- Identify patterns in responses (what's working?)

### Learning Objectives

By day 30, you should be able to:

- [ ] Explain all Lifetrek capabilities confidently
- [ ] Identify when a lead is a good fit (or not)
- [ ] Handle common objections ("too expensive", "current vendor works fine")
- [ ] Know top 10 customer pain points from conversations
- [ ] Understand competitive landscape (who we compete with, how we differentiate)
- [ ] Use all 6 email templates effectively with personalization

### Problems You Might Encounter

**Problem:** Leads aren't responding to emails
- **Solution to explore:** Test different subject lines, send times, personalization depth

**Problem:** Can't answer technical questions
- **Solution to explore:** Create internal FAQ, shadow engineering calls, build knowledge base

**Problem:** Don't know how to qualify leads
- **Solution to explore:** Develop qualification framework (BANT, MEDDIC, custom), test with team

**Ownership mindset:** Don't wait to be told what to do. If you see a gap, propose a solution.

---

## Days 31-60: Pipeline Building

### Core Problem to Solve

**Create a consistent, predictable pipeline of qualified opportunities.**

**Why this matters:**
- Inconsistent pipeline = inconsistent results
- You need systems, not just hustle
- Quality > quantity, but you need enough volume

### Success Metrics

**Activity Metrics:**
- [ ] 100+ additional leads contacted (150 total)
- [ ] 25+ qualified conversations (35 total)
- [ ] 8+ meetings scheduled (10 total)
- [ ] 4+ proposals submitted (5 total)

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Lead-to-conversation: >= 20%
- Conversation-to-meeting: >= 25%
- Meeting-to-proposal: >= 50%

**Business Metrics:**
- [ ] 1+ deal closed (any size counts!)
- Pipeline value: $50K+ in qualified opportunities

### How You'll Spend Your Time

**Daily Activities:**
- **Strategic Research** (1 hour): 3 deep-dive target accounts
  - ABM (account-based marketing) approach
  - Multi-threaded outreach (find 2-3 contacts per company)
  
- **Outreach** (1.5 hours): 8 personalized emails
  - Mix of new leads + warm follow-ups
  - Testing different messaging angles
  
- **Conversations** (2-3 hours): 2 customer calls/meetings per week
  - Discovery calls
  - Technical deep-dives
  - Proposal reviews
  
- **Pipeline Management** (30 min): Update CRM, plan next steps

**Weekly:**
- Pipeline review: Forecast next 30 days
- Content collaboration: Suggest LinkedIn topics based on customer conversations
- Process improvement: Document what's working, iterate on what's not

### Learning Objectives

By day 60, you should be able to:

- [ ] Run a discovery call independently (no hand-holding)
- [ ] Qualify or disqualify a lead in first conversation
- [ ] Understand full sales cycle from lead to closed deal
- [ ] Present Lifetrek capabilities to a technical audience
- [ ] Handle pricing conversations (when to involve manager/engineering)
- [ ] Identify early warning signs of dead-end deals

### Problems You Might Encounter

**Problem:** Meetings scheduled but not closing
- **Solution to explore:** Improve discovery process, better qualification upfront, stronger follow-up

**Problem:** Running out of good leads
- **Solution to explore:** Identify new lead sources, improve lead scoring, re-engage old leads

**Problem:** Spending too much time on small deals
- **Solution to explore:** Develop deal sizing framework, focus on high-value opportunities

## Days 61-90: Scale & Optimize

### Core Problem to Solve

**Systematize outreach and optimize conversion rates at every stage.**

**Why this matters:**
- You've learned what works - now make it repeatable
- Small improvements in conversion = big impact on results
- Systems let you  scale without burning out

### Success Metrics

**Activity Metrics:**
- [ ] 200+ additional leads contacted (350 total)
- [ ] 50+ qualified conversations (85 total)
- [ ] 15+ meetings scheduled (25 total)
- [ ] 8+ proposals submitted (13 total)

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Lead-to-conversation: >= 25%
- Conversation-to-meeting: >= 30%
- Meeting-to-proposal: >= 50%
- Proposal-to-close: >= 25%

**Business Metrics:**
- [ ] 2+ deals closed (3 total from start)
- Pipeline value: $150K+ in qualified opportunities
- Average deal size: Identify and document

### How You'll Spend Your Time

**Daily Activities:**
- **Account-Based Outreach** (1.5 hours): 5 target accounts
  - Multi-channel: email + LinkedIn + phone
  - Coordinated campaign approach
  
- **Outreach Mix** (1 hour): 10 emails
  - 5 new leads (cold)
  - 5 nurture/follow-up (warm)
  
- **Customer Meetings** (2-4 hours): 3-4 calls per week
  - Mix of discovery, technical, proposal review
  
- **Content & Enablement** (1 hour/week):
  - Collaborate on LinkedIn content
  - Build internal resources (playbooks, FAQs, case studies)

**Weekly:**
- Pipeline forecast: Predict next 60-90 days
- Process optimization workshop: What can we improve?
- Knowledge sharing: Teach team what you've learned

### Learning Objectives

By day 90, you should be able to:

- [ ] Run full sales cycle independently (discovery â†’ close)
- [ ] Train someone else on your process
- [ ] Identify patterns in closed vs. lost deals
- [ ] Contribute to improving company's sales process
- [ ] Have repeatable talk tracks for top 5 use cases
- [ ] Handle complex technical objections with confidence

### Problems You Might Encounter

**Problem:** Hitting a plateau in conversions
- **Solution to explore:** A/B test messaging, try new channels, get customer feedback

**Problem:** Too many deals, can't manage all
- **Solution to explore:** Better qualification, delegate/automate admin work, prioritize ruthlessly

**Problem:** Deals stalling in pipeline
- **Solution to explore:** Improve follow-up cadence, create urgency, address hidden objections

## Monthly Check-Ins with Manager

### What We'll Review

**Pipeline Health:**
- How many opportunities at each stage?
- Are deals moving forward or stuck?
- Forecast accuracy (are you predicting well?)

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Where are you strongest? (celebrate wins)
- Where are drop-offs happening? (diagnose issues)

**Customer Feedback:**
- What are you hearing in conversations?
- Common themes in objections?
- Market insights?

**Process Improvements:**
- What's working well?
- What's not working?
- Ideas to test?

**Support Needed:**
- Where are you stuck?
- What resources would help?
- Who should you meet/shadow?
